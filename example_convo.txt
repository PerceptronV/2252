⏺ Created /Users/hanmingye/Documents/2252/spectral_embedding.py and verified it
  runs.

  What's in the file:
  - normalized_laplacian(A) — builds sparse L_sym = I − D^{-1/2} A D^{-1/2},
  handles isolated nodes by setting their D^{-1/2} entry to 0.
  - spectral_embedding(A, k, row_normalize=False, ...) — returns a
  SpectralEmbedding dataclass with the k smallest eigenvalues, the n×k
  eigenvector matrix, and the degree vector.
  - Accepts dense or sparse adjacency, defensively symmetrizes, zeros the
  diagonal.

  One implementation note worth flagging: I'm computing the largest eigenvalues
  of M = 2I − L_sym rather than calling eigsh directly with which='SM' or
  sigma=0. Reason: which='SM' is numerically unreliable for ARPACK on
  Laplacians, and sigma=0 shift-invert needs a factorization that fails when the
   graph is disconnected (L_sym is exactly singular). The 2I − L_sym trick gives
   the same eigenvectors and lets ARPACK work on the easy end of the spectrum.

  Sanity check output (two 20-node cliques bridged by one edge):
  - Bottom-3 eigenvalues: [0.0, 0.0048, 1.003] — the second eigenvalue is tiny,
  exactly what Cheeger predicts for a near-disconnected graph.
  - Fiedler vector cleanly splits 20/20.
